What is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases (including carbon dioxide and methane) that are generated by our actions.

In the process of reducing the carbon footprint, both individuals and institutions have important tasks, but a better understanding of what happened before taking action also allows you to understand how important it is. Here's what's curious about the carbon footprint…

Carbon footprint greenhouse gas carbon dioxide measured in terms of the amount of volume as a measure of damage to the environment and human activities consists of two main parts: direct (primary) and indirect footprint (secondary) footprint.

Carbon footprint is a term used to describe the amount of carbon that each person causes to spread into the atmosphere as a result of transportation, heating, energy consumption, or any product they buy.

The primary footprint of domestic energy consumption and transportation (cars and planes) of CO2 emissions from the burning of fossil fuels, including the entire life cycle of the products we use a secondary footprint of these products is a measure of the indirect CO2 emissions related to production and eventually decay.

What is Global Warming?

As a result of the greenhouse effect, which is thought to be caused by the increase in heat-holding gases such as carbon dioxide in the atmosphere and the greenhouse gases released into the atmosphere, global warming is the name given to the increase in average temperatures measured in land, sea and air throughout the year on earth. Today, climate scientists (climatologists) agree on global warming.

Global Climate Change Is climate change caused by global warming caused by greenhouse gases, whose amount and density in the atmosphere increases as a result of human consumption of fossil fuels, industrial and agricultural activities. These climate changes include drought, desertification, imbalance and deviations in rainfall, flooding, typhoons, storms, tornadoes, etc. it is manifested by symptoms such as increases in meteorological events.

Recycling

Recycling is the Re-production and production of unused wastes as raw materials by various recycling methods. First of all, the need for raw materials is reduced by the fact that the consumed substances can participate in the recycling ring again. In this way, increasing consumption in parallel with the increase of the human population is prevented from disturbing the natural balance and damaging nature. However, re-use of recyclable materials as raw materials saves a large amount of energy.

According to research, using recoverable aluminum saves up to 35% of energy compared to manufacturing aluminum from scratch.

The use of waste materials as raw materials is also extremely important in terms of preventing environmental pollution. Re-use of used paper in paper production can reduce air pollution by 74-94%, water pollution by 35% and water use by 45%. For example, cutting down 8 trees can be prevented by adding a ton of waste paper to the pulp.
